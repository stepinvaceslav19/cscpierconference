Electronic Visa to Russia for Foreign Citizens

Key Provisions üìå

Since August 1, 2023, the Russian Federation has introduced a unified electronic visa system available to citizens of 64 countries.
Since August 23, 2025, the conditions for obtaining an electronic visa have been improved:
  • Visa validity period ‚Äî up to 120 days from the date of issue (previously 60 days)
  • Permitted length of stay ‚Äî up to 30 days from the date of entry (previously 16 days)
  • Visa type ‚Äî single-entry

Citizens of 64 foreign countries are eligible to apply for an electronic visa.

Processing Time ‚è≥

  • Application processing time ‚Äî up to 4 calendar days
  • The application must be submitted no later than 4 calendar days before the planned date of entry into Russia

Required Documents üìÑ

To obtain an electronic visa, the following documents are required:
  • A digital facial photograph
  • A scanned copy of the passport page containing personal data
Important: The passport must be valid for at least 6 months from the date of entry into the Russian Federation.

Cost üí≥

  • For adults ‚Äî the consular fee is included in the visa processing service fee
  • For children under 6 years old ‚Äî the electronic visa is issued free of charge

Medical Insurance ü©∫

Foreign citizens entering Russia with a unified electronic visa must have valid medical insurance covering the entire period of stay in the Russian Federation.

Points of Entry ‚úàÔ∏è

The electronic visa is valid only for entry through designated border checkpoints.
In Moscow, the following air checkpoints are available:
  • Moscow (Vnukovo)
  • Moscow (Domodedovo)
  • Moscow (Sheremetyevo)
  • Moscow (Zhukovsky)
Entry with an electronic visa is also possible through more than 100 checkpoints across Russia, including:
  • Road checkpoints
  • Railway checkpoints
  • Seaports

Purpose of Visit üéØ

The unified electronic visa allows entry into the Russian Federation for:
  • Private or business visits
  • Tourism
  • Participation in scientific, cultural, socio-political, economic, and sporting events

Terms and Procedures for Access
to GPI RAS for In-Person Participants
Passage to the GPI RAS for full-time (offline) participation is possible only for Russian citizens and is carried out according to the lists at the security point at the checkpoint of the GPI RAS upon presentation of a passport of a citizen of the Russian Federation.
The list for passage includes all participants who indicated the offline participation format during registration or declared it when contacting the organizing committee. Most of the participants, citizens of Russia, from Moscow, St. Petersburg and the cities closest to them, who indicated the form of participation online, are also included in the list for access to the GPI RAS and can clarify this by contacting the organizing committee by e-mail cscpier@mail.ru.

Foreign citizens who have received special permission are admitted according to the access lists at the security checkpoint upon presentation of an identity document, a copy of which was submitted during the permission application process.

Participants who are citizens of the Russian Federation and were not previously registered in the application forms but requested access passes will be able to collect them at the GPI RAS Pass Office from 9:00 to 16:00. Pass issuance usually takes 10‚Äì15 minutes.
